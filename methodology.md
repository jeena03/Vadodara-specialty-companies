# Vadodara-specialty-companies
List of 25 high-potential specialty manufacturing companies in Vadodara with scoring

## Research Methodology

Company Identification and Scoring — Vadodara Chemicals and Industrial Sector

Prepared by: Jeena Anandita  
Date: 4 May 2026  
Universe: 25 Companies
---
### 1. Objective

The goal of this exercise was to identify, profile, and score 25 companies operating in Vadodara’s chemical, pharmaceutical, and industrial manufacturing space. Each company was evaluated across six criteria: business type, India HQ confirmation, differentiation, technical leadership, sector tailwinds, and active growth signals.

The final output is a scored CSV with one-line evidence for each criterion, built to support a business development or investment pipeline.

### 2. How I Found the Companies

I did not start with a fixed list. The process was bottom-up, beginning with broad directories and narrowing down into specific segments.

#### 2.1 Primary Discovery Sources

- VCCI member directory helped identify registered manufacturers and exporters.

- Vadodara industry directories, both print and online, provided access to smaller firms not always visible on LinkedIn or IndiaMART.

- LinkedIn search was used with filters for Vadodara and relevant industries, and employee profiles helped validate company details.

- IndiaMART and TradeIndia were useful for identifying smaller firms and understanding actual product offerings.

- Google search with targeted queries helped surface niche manufacturers.

- Property and commercial listing platforms helped verify whether a company operated from an industrial zone or a commercial office.

- Glassdoor and AmbitionBox were used to estimate company size and hiring activity.

#### 2.2 Cross-Verification Sources

- Tofler and Zauba Corp were used to verify incorporation details, NIC codes, directors, and financial filings.

- CPHI Online helped validate certifications and regulatory approvals for pharma companies.

- Screener and Trendlyne were used for listed company data such as revenue and growth trends.

- Google Scholar and the Indian Patent Office were used to check technical credibility and innovation.

- DSIR database helped identify companies with recognized R&D units.

- AI tools like Claude and Grok were used for quick summaries, but all information was cross-verified before use.

### 3. Research Process

For each company, I followed a consistent sequence:

- Basic identity was verified through MCA-linked sources.

- Business type was confirmed using NIC codes, directories, and company websites.

- Location was checked to ensure India presence and Vadodara operations.

- Products were reviewed to understand what the company actually makes.

- Differentiation was assessed through patents, certifications, or unique positioning.

- Decision makers were identified and their background was verified through LinkedIn and other sources.

- Sector tailwinds were mapped to PLI, China+1, Make in India, and policy support.

- Growth signals were checked through hiring, updates, expansion, and financial indicators.

### 4. Scoring Approach

- Each criterion was scored as Weak, Moderate, or Strong based on a predefined framework. The scoring logic was fixed beforehand to maintain consistency.

- One key decision was to score the company itself, not just the sector. For example, a distributor operating in a growing sector was not given full credit if it did not directly benefit from those tailwinds.

- For growth signals, only verifiable and recent evidence was considered. Generic claims or outdated information were not included.

### 5. Limitations

- Many smaller firms lacked reliable public data, especially around revenue and leadership background.

- Director qualification data was inconsistent and often incomplete across sources.

- Growth signals for private companies were harder to verify compared to listed firms.

- In cases of conflicting information across platforms, decisions were made based on the strongest available evidence.

### 6. What I Learned

- Vadodara has a diverse industrial base beyond petrochemicals, including APIs, specialty chemicals, water treatment, and engineering firms.

- Companies with strong differentiation usually had either technical founders or recognized R&D setups.

- Many traders present themselves as manufacturers, making verification critical.

- Government-driven demand, especially from research institutions, plays a major role.

- China+1 opportunities exist but are not fully captured by smaller firms yet.
